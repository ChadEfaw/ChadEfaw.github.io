GIS and Spatial Analysis

I have intentionally tailored my assignments towards environmental conservation using statistics and GIS-informed decision-making. For example, I identified a natural experiment in that two coal-fired power plants in West Virginia – owned by the same company – had distinct environmental control systems. Are there statistically significant epidemiological differences in health outcomes of employees at each site? To answer that question, I designed a survey instrument, data collection and analysis plan. Second, I completed a spatial-temporal analysis of fossil fuel production in West Virginia from 1970 to 2020. This analysis allowed me to track the moving “center of gravity” of extraction and production – as well as the evolving modal production type: coal and then natural natural gas – over time throughout the state. Are current regulations equipped to protect human health and wellbeing when the mode of resource extraction changes – in space, time, and type? Ethics

I have written about environmental ethics The most important professional experience of my life was being (unofficially) part of the Pittsburgh Task Force on Public Algorithms .
