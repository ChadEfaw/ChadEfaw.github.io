GIS and Spatial Analysis

I have intentionally tailored my assignments towards environmental conservation using statistics and GIS-informed decision-making. For example, I identified a natural experiment in that two coal-fired power plants in West Virginia – owned by the same company – had distinct environmental control systems. Are there statistically significant epidemiological differences in health outcomes of employees at each site? To answer that question, I designed a survey instrument, data collection and analysis plan. Second, I completed a spatial-temporal analysis of fossil fuel production in West Virginia from 1970 to 2020. This analysis allowed me to track the moving “center of gravity” of extraction and production – as well as the evolving modal production type: coal and then natural natural gas – over time throughout the state. Are current regulations equipped to protect human health and wellbeing when the mode of resource extraction changes – in space, time, and type? Economics and Finance

In addition to GIS and Spatial Analysis, I have also had many opportunities to write on economics and finance, specifically related to clean energy.
For example, I performed a financial analysis of First Solar, the largest solar panel manufacturer in the United States.

Combining spatial analysis with public finance, I completed a Clean energy Investment Paper for a graduate course in economics.

Finally, I am always interested in bringing these skills back home so for a public budgeting course, I analyzed the municipal finances of a city in West Virginia. This project analyzed Fayetteville, West Virginia's municipal finances in comparison with other similarly-sized towns. I wrote code in R to scrape budgets from the West Virginia Secretary of State's office website

Ethics
