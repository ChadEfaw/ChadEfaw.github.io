In January 2024, my City Council member resigned, leaving seat open for an unexpired term. With my prior experience speaking in front of the Council, I decided to apply and interview for the open seat. In preparation for the interview I created a spreadsheet and tabulated every action the Council took in the last calendar year.
This allowed me a more global understanding of:
1. What the Council actually does and what is (and is not) on its institutional agenda;
2. Who on the Council takes what action; more importantly, who motions particular Council actions and who seconds them.     This is provides a rough estimate of coalitions within the institution.

Cultivate Fairmont

After interviewing for the open Council seat, I let it be known that I was interested in getting more involved in the City. A few months later, I was asked to participate in the application process for Cultivate West Virginia, a program affiliated with the Community Development Hub of West Virginia. In April, 2024, Fairmont was awarded the designation, funding, and coaching and started Cultivate Fairmont This program will spend the next 12 months facilitating feedback from the public; identifying community needs; and allocating $50,000 to various projects around the city.
